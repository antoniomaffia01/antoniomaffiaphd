About

I build the wet-lab platforms that make gene therapy programs run faster, smarter, and closer to the clinic.

Over 10+ years in academic and biotech R&D, I've learned that the hardest part of drug discovery isn't generating data — it's generating the data at the scale machine learning actually needs. That's where I sit: at the intersection of high-throughput wet-lab screening and AI/ML-driven therapeutic optimization, building the pipelines that let both sides move at their natural pace.

At EARLI, I engineer synthetic promoters for tumor-selective gene expression, lead MPRA screening campaigns of >100,000 regulatory elements, and scale non-viral therapeutic vector pipelines — all feeding into AI-guided design cycles. Previously, I helped build Mammoth Biosciences' CRISPR screening platform from scratch and led a multi-year pharma partnership with GSK at UC Berkeley. I also advise early-stage gene therapy startups on scientific strategy and platform decisions.

If you're working on gene therapy, synthetic biology, or AI-integrated drug discovery — and you need someone who has done this from academia to pre-seed startup to biotech — let's talk.
