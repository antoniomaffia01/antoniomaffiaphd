At Mammoth Biosciences, I developed innovative lentiviral-based pooled screening workflows that revolutionized protein engineering campaigns, delivering exponential increases in screening capabilities. Leading the entire screening pipeline, I orchestrated experimental design, timeline management, cross-functional team coordination, and resource allocation. I pioneered novel library cloning strategies and engineered sophisticated cell-based functional assays through precision genetic engineering. This experience strengthened my expertise in project management and leadership while advancing my knowledge of protein engineering for cell therapy applications.
My research at UC Berkeley created a groundbreaking stem cell-based platform for cancer drug discovery through advanced genetic engineering of human stem cells. This innovative approach provides a more efficient and time-saving method for generating cancer models in vitro, unlocking new possibilities for functional genomics studies to identify novel druggable targets. Through this work, I developed deep expertise in genetic editing of complex cell lines and functional genomics, while collaborating with exceptional scientists who reinforced my commitment to cross-functional teamwork.
During my graduate studies at the University of Pavia, I authored multiple publications in cancer biology, focusing on DNA repair and cellular replication—pathways critical to both cancer development and chemoresistance. My research contributed to identifying key therapeutic targets that can be leveraged for future cancer treatments. These foundational years established my expertise in cancer biology and biochemistry while igniting my passion for genetic engineering, ultimately shaping my career trajectory in biotechnology.
