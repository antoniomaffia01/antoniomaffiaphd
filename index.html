Experience & Education Timeline

May 2023 – Nov 2024 · Senior Scientist I, Mammoth Biosciences

At Mammoth Biosciences I built different lentiviral-based pooled screening workflows that supported protein engineering campaigns unlocking an exponential increase in screening capabilities. I managed the entire screening workflow taking charge of the experimental design, timelines management, cross-teams co-ordination and resource management. I have technically developed libraries cloning strategies and cell-based functional assays through targeted genetic engineering.

This experience has allowed me to gain more expertise in project management and leadership and has solidified my knowledge of protein engineering in the field of cell therapy.

Mar 2020 – May 2023 · Research Scientist, UC Berkeley

My work at UC Berkeley produced a stem cells-based genetically engineered new platform for cancer drug testing. By complex genetic engineering of human stem cells this breakthrough work has provided a more efficient and time saving method to generate cancer models in vitro. This has unlocked the possibility to perform functional genomics studies to discover novel druggable targets as possible targets for cancer treatment.

Thanks to this work I have acquired a strong expertise in genetic editing in complex cell lines and functional genomics. I have had the luck to work with brilliant collaborators and have fortified my belief in cross-functional collaborations.

Oct 2018 – Feb 2020 · Research Scientist, Institute of Molecular Genetics

At the Institute of Molecular Genetics in Pavia, I conducted independent research focused on DNA repair and cell cycle regulation. I engineered CRISPR-based cellular models and led high-throughput screening of chemical compounds targeting DNA-structure elements. My work contributed to identifying key modulators of genomic stability relevant to cancer therapy.

I also mentored students and colleagues, enhancing my collaborative and leadership capabilities while furthering my passion for mechanistic cancer research.
